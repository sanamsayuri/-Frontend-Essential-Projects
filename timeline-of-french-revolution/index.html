Timeline of French Revolution

1.1788 – The royal treasury is empty; Prelude to the Revolution

2.1789 – The Revolution Begins; the Estates-General and the Constituent Assembly

3.July 14, 1789 – The Siege and Surrender of the Bastille

4.August 27, 1789 – Declaration of the Rights of Man and of the Citizen

5.October 6, 1789 – Women's March on Versailles

6.1790 – the Rise of the Political Clubs

7.July 14, 1790 – Fête de la Fédération

8.1791 – The unsuccessful flight of the Royal Family from Paris

9.June 20–21, 1791 – The Royal Family flees Paris

10.1792 – War and the overthrow of the monarchy

11.August 10, 1792 – Storming of the Tuileries; Downfall of the King

12.September 2–7, 1792 – Massacres in Paris prisons

13.September 20, 1792 – French victory at Valmy; Debut of the Convention

14.December 10, 1792 – January 21, 1793 – Trial and Execution of Louis XVI

15.1793 – France at war against Europe; The Jacobins seize power; The Terror begins

16.April 6 – May 30, 1793 - Committee on Public Safety takes control of government

17.May 31 – June 2, 1793 – The Jacobin Coup d'État

18.July 13, 1793 – Assassination of Jean-Paul Marat by Charlotte Corday

19.September 17, 1793 – The Reign of Terror begins

20.October 16, 1793 – The execution of Marie-Antoinette

21.1794 – The fury of the Terror or, the Cult of the Supreme Being, and the Downfall of Robespierre

22.March 30, 1794 – The arrest and trial of Danton and Desmoulins

23.May 10, 1794 - Louis XVI's Sister, Madame Élisabeth is Executed

24.June 8, 1794 – Festival of the Supreme Being; Acceleration of the Terror

25.July 26–28, 1794 – Arrest and execution of Robespierre; End of the Terror

26.1795 – The Directory Replaces the Convention

27.May 20–24, 1795 – Last Paris uprising by the Jacobins and sans-culottes

28.June 25 – July 27, 1795 – Renewed uprisings in the Vendée and a royalist invasion of Brittany

29.August 22 – September 23, 1795 – The new Constitution is approved: the Directory takes power

30.October 5, 1795 – "A whiff of grapeshot": General Bonaparte suppresses a royalist rebellion in Paris

31.1796 – Napoleon's campaign in Italy; Defeat of the royalists in the Vendée; a failed uprising in Paris
